Laboratory Examination Methods

Microscopic Examination

3.1.1 Gram Staining

Primary classification method for bacteria based on cell wall structure

  • Gram Positive: Appears blue/purple (thick cell wall)
  • Gram Negative: Appears red/pink (thin cell wall)
  • Most important staining technique in bacteriology

Special stain to detect acid-fast bacteria

  • Mycobacterium leprae (Leprosy)
  • Bacteria appear red on blue background

Culturing the Bacteria

3.2.1 General Purpose Media

Media that support growth of most non-fastidious bacteria

  • Nutrient Agar: Basic medium containing peptone and meat extract
  • Nutrient Broth: Liquid version of Nutrient Agar
  • Used for routine cultivation and storage

3.2.2 Enriched Media

Nutrient-rich media for fastidious bacteria

  • Blood Agar: Contains 5% blood (sheep or horse)
  • Chocolate Agar: Heat-lysed blood
  • Used for sensitive bacteria like Streptococcus and Haemophilus

  • Mannitol Salt Agar: Selective for Staphylococci
  • Salmonella-Shigella Agar: Selective for Salmonella and Shigella

Serological Tests

About Serological Tests

Serological tests detect antibodies or antigens to identify bacterial infections. These tests are based on antigen-antibody reactions and are crucial for diagnosing infections where the pathogen is difficult to culture or when rapid diagnosis is needed.

  • ELISA: Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay
  • Agglutination Tests: Direct and Indirect
  • Immunofluorescence: Direct and Indirect IF
  • Western Blot: Protein detection method

PCR - Polymerase Chain Reaction

About PCR

DNA amplification technique for bacterial identification at molecular level. PCR is one of the most powerful tools in modern microbiology, allowing for rapid and accurate detection of bacterial pathogens even in small quantities.

  • Real-Time PCR (qPCR): Quantitative detection
  • Multiplex PCR: Multiple targets simultaneously
  • Reverse Transcription PCR: For RNA detection
  • Nested PCR: Enhanced specificity

Phage Typing

About Phage Typing

Using bacteriophages (bacterial viruses) to identify and classify bacterial strains. This method exploits the specificity of bacteriophages for particular bacterial strains, making it useful for epidemiological studies and outbreak investigations.

  • Principle: Specific phages lyse specific bacterial strains
  • Applications: Epidemiological investigations
  • Advantages: High specificity and reproducibility
  • Common uses: Staphylococcus aureus, Salmonella typing
